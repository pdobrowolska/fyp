<h1> Description </h1>
<p></p>Since February 2022, over 120,000 Ukrainians have crossed the Irish border as Beneficiaries of Temporary Protection. Despite the scale of this migration, the role of Irish online media in shaping public perceptions of Ukrainian Refugees has remained largely unexplored. This project investigates how the leading news outlets have contributed to the social construction through analysing articles shared on X (formerly Twitter). Using X as a data source, a dataset was collected and subsequently analysed utilising methods of Natural Language Processing to identify patterns in language and sentiment. The computational approach was complemented by Critical Discourse Analysis, which provided a sociological framework for better understanding the narratives. The project aims to explore how media discourse may reflect or reinforce forms of racism, including anti-Slavic racism, and potential anti-refugee sentiments in the specific context of Ukrainian refugees in Ireland. It also considers how these narratives may intersect with broader socio-economic pressures, such as the cost of living crisis, or the housing crisis.</p>
